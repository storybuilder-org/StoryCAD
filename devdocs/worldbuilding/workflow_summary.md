# Collaborator Workflow Summary

| Label | Title | Category | Description |
|-------|-------|----------|-------------|
| Premise | Ideation (Story idea => Concept => Premise) | Overview | The goal of this workflow is to ensure that a workable premise has been created, usually from the idea and concept, or from a story prompt. |
| StoryProblem | Story Problem (Premise => Problem + Characters) | Overview | Transform a developed Premise into a complete Story Problem with linked Protagonist and Antagonist characters. |
| StoryForm | Story Form | Overview | Define the story's genre, length, and structural form. |
| GMC | Goal / Motivation / Conflict (GMC) | Problem | The goal of this workflow is to ensure that a Problem Story Element is a well-formed problem capable of contributing to the story's plot. |
| ConflictBuilder | Conflict Builder | Problem | Use the Conflict Builder tool to develop and intensify the central conflict of a story problem, exploring different conflict categories and escalation patterns. |
| InnerOuterProblems | Inner and Outer Problems | Problem | Given an outer (external goal) problem, develop a complementary inner problem representing the protagonist's internal struggle—rooted in a flaw or wound that must be overcome before the outer problem can truly be resolved. |
| Structure | Problem Structure | Problem | Define the structural beats and turning points of a story problem by selecting and applying a beat sheet template. |
| ComplicationBuilder | Complication Builder | Problem | Generate complications and obstacles that raise stakes, escalate conflict, and build toward your story's climax. |
| RoleAndStoryRole | Role and Story Role | Character | Define a character's archetypal role and their function in the story. |
| PhysicalAppearance | Physical and Appearance | Character | Develop a character's physical description, distinctive features, and overall appearance that readers will visualize. |
| SocialFactors | Social Background | Character | Explore a character's social background, economic status, education, ethnicity, and relationship to society. |
| PsychologicalMakeup | Psychological Profile | Character | Develop a character's personality, psychology, values, and mental characteristics using the Enneagram and other frameworks. |
| InnerOuterTraits | Inner and Outer Traits | Character | Define the visible behaviors others see (outer traits) and the hidden inner qualities that drive them (inner traits). |
| Flaw | Character Flaw | Character | Identify and develop a character's central flaw—the weakness or blind spot tied to their inner problem and character arc. |
| Backstory | Backstory | Character | Develop the character's formative history—the wound, ghost, or defining events that explain who they are now. |
| Relationship | Character Relationship | Character | Develop the dynamics, history, and tension between two characters. |
| SettingTimeSpace | Setting in Time and Space | Setting | Define a setting's location, time period, season, weather, and atmospheric conditions. |
| Sensations | Sensory Details | Setting | Develop the sensory details—sights, sounds, touch, smell, and taste—that bring a setting to life. |
| SettingCreateImage | Create Setting Image | Setting | Generate a visual representation of a setting using AI image generation. |
| WorldRulesLogic | World Rules and Logic | World Model | Define the fundamental rules and internal logic of your story world—what is possible, impossible, and how things work. |
| WorldHistory | World History and Background | World Model | Develop the historical context and background events that shaped your story world into its current state. |
| GeographyCultures | Geography and Cultures | World Model | Define the physical geography and cultural groups that populate your story world. |
| TechnologyMagic | Technology and Magic Systems | World Model | Design the technological or magical systems that shape your story world, including their rules, limits, and costs. |
| SceneSummary | Scene Summary | Scene | Create a concise summary of a scene's purpose, content, and role in the larger story. |
| CastSceneRoles | Cast and Scene Roles | Scene | Define which characters appear in a scene and what role each plays—protagonist, antagonist, ally, or other function. |
| SceneDevelopment | Scene Development | Scene | Develop how a scene advances both the outer plot problem and the protagonist's inner character arc. |
| SceneConflict | Scene Conflict | Scene | Structure the conflict within a scene—the protagonist's goal, the opposition they face, and the outcome. |
| Sequel | Sequel (Reaction) | Scene | Develop the character's emotional reaction, reflection, and decision-making after a scene's conflict. |
| SceneCreateImage | Create Scene Image | Scene | Generate a visual representation of a scene using AI image generation. |

**26 workflows total**
