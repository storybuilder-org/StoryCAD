# World Type Examples

**Created:** 2026-01-19
**Purpose:** Concrete examples to help writers understand each World Type

---

## Why Examples Matter

Abstract categories become clear through familiar examples. Series and shared-world fiction are especially valuable because they demonstrate sustained worldbuilding across multiple works.

---

## 1. Consensus Reality

> The world works as expected. No hidden rules.

### Defining Characteristics
- No speculative elements
- Real-world physics and society
- Drama comes from human conflict, not world mechanics

### Literary Fiction
- John Updike's **Rabbit** series (Rabbit, Run; Rabbit Redux; Rabbit Is Rich; Rabbit at Rest)
- Jonathan Franzen's novels (*The Corrections*, *Freedom*)
- Donna Tartt (*The Secret History*, *The Goldfinch*)

### Crime/Mystery Series
- Ed McBain's **87th Precinct** novels (55 books)
- Robert B. Parker's **Spenser** series
- **Jesse Stone** novels and TV movies (Parker/Brandman)
- Michael Connelly's **Harry Bosch** series
- Sue Grafton's **Kinsey Millhone** alphabet series

### Thrillers
- John Grisham legal thrillers
- Tom Clancy's **Jack Ryan** series
- Lee Child's **Jack Reacher** series

### Romance
- Nicholas Sparks novels
- Nora Roberts contemporary romance

### Historical Fiction
- Hilary Mantel's **Wolf Hall** trilogy
- Ken Follett's **Pillars of the Earth** series
- Patrick O'Brian's **Aubrey-Maturin** series

## 2. Enchanted Reality

> Our world, but reality is porous. The impossible is accepted, not analyzed.

### Defining Characteristics
- Magic exists but isn't systematized or explained
- Characters accept the impossible as part of life
- Tone is often literary, symbolic, emotional

### Literary Magical Realism
- Gabriel García Márquez - **One Hundred Years of Solitude**, *Love in the Time of Cholera*
- Isabel Allende - **The House of the Spirits**
- Laura Esquivel - **Like Water for Chocolate**
- Toni Morrison - **Beloved**, *Song of Solomon*
- Salman Rushdie - *Midnight's Children*, *The Satanic Verses*

### Contemporary Magical Realism
- Alice Hoffman - *Practical Magic*, *The Rules of Magic*
- Joanne Harris - *Chocolat*
- Sarah Addison Allen - *Garden Spells*

### Film
- **Pan's Labyrinth** (del Toro)
- **The Shape of Water** (del Toro)
- **Big Fish** (Burton)
- **Amélie** (Jeunet)
- **Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon** (Lee)

### Television
- **Pushing Daisies**
- **Dead Like Me**

---

## 3. Hidden World

> Our world + concealed layers. Discovery is dangerous or costly.

### Defining Characteristics
- Secret magical society exists alongside mundane world
- Masquerade maintained (hidden from ordinary people)
- Protagonist often discovers the hidden layer

### Wizarding/Magic Schools
- J.K. Rowling's **Harry Potter** series (7 books + expanded universe)
- Lev Grossman's **The Magicians** trilogy
- V.E. Schwab's **Shades of Magic** series

### Urban Fantasy Series
- Jim Butcher's **Dresden Files** (17+ books)
- Patricia Briggs' **Mercy Thompson** series
- Ilona Andrews' **Kate Daniels** series
- Kevin Hearne's **Iron Druid Chronicles**
- Laurell K. Hamilton's **Anita Blake** series
- Charlaine Harris' **Sookie Stackhouse** / True Blood

### Mythology in Modern World
- Neil Gaiman - **American Gods**, *Anansi Boys*
- Rick Riordan's **Percy Jackson** series and expanded universe
- Madeline Miller - *Circe* (mythic but set in hidden divine realm)

### Secret History
- Umberto Eco - *Foucault's Pendulum*
- Dan Brown's **Robert Langdon** series (*The Da Vinci Code*, etc.)
- Katherine Arden's **Winternight** trilogy

### Film/TV
- **Men in Black** series
- **The Matrix** trilogy (technological hidden world)
- **Buffy the Vampire Slayer** / **Angel**
- **Supernatural** (15 seasons)
- **Grimm**
- **The Umbrella Academy**

## 4. Divergent World

> History or conditions forked. Logic remains rational.

### Defining Characteristics
- "What if?" premise changes history or near-future
- No magic; science may differ
- Rational extrapolation from divergence point

### Alternate History
- Philip K. Dick - **The Man in the High Castle**
- Stephen King - **11/22/63**
- Philip Roth - *The Plot Against America*
- Jo Walton - **Small Change** trilogy
- Harry Turtledove - multiple alternate history series

### Steampunk (Divergent + Aesthetic)
- William Gibson & Bruce Sterling - *The Difference Engine*
- Cherie Priest - **Clockwork Century** series
- Gail Carriger - **Parasol Protectorate** series

### Cyberpunk
- William Gibson - **Sprawl** trilogy (*Neuromancer*, etc.)
- Neal Stephenson - *Snow Crash*, *The Diamond Age*
- Richard K. Morgan - **Altered Carbon** series

### Near-Future SF
- Margaret Atwood - **The Handmaid's Tale** / *The Testaments*
- Kazuo Ishiguro - *Never Let Me Go*
- Andy Weir - *The Martian*, *Project Hail Mary*

### Film/TV
- **Blade Runner** / **Blade Runner 2049**
- **Black Mirror** (anthology)
- **The Man in the High Castle** (TV)
- **For All Mankind**
- **Watchmen** (alternate 1985)

## 5. Constructed World

> Entirely built reality. Coherent internal logic.

### Defining Characteristics
- Fully invented world (not Earth)
- Author-defined rules and systems
- Often includes detailed history, cultures, magic systems

### Epic Fantasy
- George R.R. Martin - **A Song of Ice and Fire** (Game of Thrones)
- Brandon Sanderson - **Cosmere** universe (*Stormlight Archive*, *Mistborn*)
- Robert Jordan - **Wheel of Time** (14 books)
- Steven Erikson - **Malazan Book of the Fallen** (10 books)
- Robin Hobb - **Realm of the Elderlings** (16 books)

### Classic Fantasy
- Terry Pratchett - **Discworld** (41 books) - whimsical tone
- Ursula K. Le Guin - **Earthsea** (also Mythic)
- Raymond E. Feist - **Riftwar** saga

### Science Fiction (Secondary World)
- Frank Herbert - **Dune** series
- Becky Chambers - **Wayfarers** series
- Ann Leckie - **Imperial Radch** trilogy

### Science Fantasy
- **Star Wars** universe (films, books, TV)
- Gene Wolfe - **Book of the New Sun**

### Shared Worlds
- **Forgotten Realms** (D&D)
- **Warhammer 40,000**
- **Star Trek** universe

### Film/TV
- **The Lord of the Rings** / **The Hobbit** films
- **Game of Thrones** / **House of the Dragon**
- **The Witcher**
- **Wheel of Time** (TV)
- **Avatar** (Cameron) - Pandora

## 6. Mythic World

> Reality follows meaning, not causality. Fate, prophecy, archetypes dominate.

### Defining Characteristics
- Narrative logic supersedes physical logic
- Prophecy and destiny are real forces
- Archetypal characters and journeys
- Often feels "true" in a symbolic sense

### Foundational Mythic Fantasy
- J.R.R. Tolkien - **The Lord of the Rings**, *The Silmarillion*, *The Hobbit*
- C.S. Lewis - **The Chronicles of Narnia**
- Ursula K. Le Guin - **Earthsea** series
- T.H. White - **The Once and Future King**

### Mythology Retellings
- Madeline Miller - **Circe**, **The Song of Achilles**
- Pat Barker - **The Silence of the Girls**, *The Women of Troy*
- Stephen Fry - **Mythos**, **Heroes**, **Troy**
- Neil Gaiman - *Norse Mythology*

### Arthurian
- Mary Stewart - **Merlin** trilogy
- Marion Zimmer Bradley - **The Mists of Avalon**
- Bernard Cornwell - **Warlord Chronicles**

### Modern Mythic
- Guy Gavriel Kay - **Fionavar Tapestry**, *Tigana*, *The Lions of Al-Rassan*
- Patricia McKillip - *The Forgotten Beasts of Eld*
- Lois McMaster Bujold - **World of the Five Gods**

### Film
- **The Lord of the Rings** trilogy (Jackson)
- **Excalibur** (Boorman)
- **Princess Mononoke** (Miyazaki)
- **Spirited Away** (Miyazaki)

## 7. Estranged World

> Reality is unfamiliar and unsettling. Rules exist but resist intuition.

### Defining Characteristics
- Alienating, strange, or incomprehensible elements
- Rules exist but feel "wrong" or beyond human understanding
- Often induces cosmic dread or philosophical unease

### Hard SF (Alienating Science)
- Stanislaw Lem - **Solaris**
- Peter Watts - **Blindsight**, *Echopraxia*
- Greg Egan - *Diaspora*, *Permutation City*
- Liu Cixin - **Three-Body Problem** trilogy

### New Weird
- China Miéville - **Perdido Street Station**, *The City & The City*
- Jeff VanderMeer - **Southern Reach** trilogy (*Annihilation*)
- M. John Harrison - *Viriconium*

### Cosmic Horror
- H.P. Lovecraft - **Cthulhu Mythos**
- Thomas Ligotti - *Songs of a Dead Dreamer*
- John Langan - *The Fisherman*
- Laird Barron - horror collections

### Surreal/Absurdist
- Franz Kafka - *The Metamorphosis*, *The Trial*
- Jorge Luis Borges - *Ficciones*
- Italo Calvino - *Invisible Cities*, *If on a winter's night a traveler*

### Film
- **2001: A Space Odyssey** (Kubrick)
- **Annihilation** (Garland)
- **Arrival** (Villeneuve)
- **Under the Skin** (Glazer)
- **Stalker** (Tarkovsky)

## 8. Broken World

> Systems have failed. Survival replaces progress.

### Defining Characteristics
- Civilization collapsed or corrupted
- Scarcity, danger, struggle for survival
- Often explores what makes us human when systems fail

### Post-Apocalyptic
- Cormac McCarthy - **The Road**
- Emily St. John Mandel - **Station Eleven**
- Walter M. Miller Jr. - **A Canticle for Leibowitz**
- Justin Cronin - **The Passage** trilogy
- N.K. Jemisin - **Broken Earth** trilogy (also Constructed)

### Zombie Apocalypse
- **The Walking Dead** (comics, TV, novels)
- Max Brooks - **World War Z**

### Dystopian
- George Orwell - **1984**
- Aldous Huxley - **Brave New World**
- Ray Bradbury - **Fahrenheit 451**
- Anthony Burgess - **A Clockwork Orange**
- Suzanne Collins - **The Hunger Games** trilogy
- Veronica Roth - **Divergent** trilogy

### Climate/Ecological Collapse
- Kim Stanley Robinson - *The Ministry for the Future*
- Paolo Bacigalupi - **The Water Knife**, *The Windup Girl*
- Octavia Butler - **Parable** series

### Film/TV
- **Mad Max** series (especially *Fury Road*)
- **The Book of Eli**
- **Children of Men**
- **The Handmaid's Tale** (TV)
- **The Last of Us**
- **Snowpiercer**

## Cross-Category Examples

Some works span multiple World Types:

| Work | Primary Type | Secondary Type |
|------|--------------|----------------|
| Lord of the Rings | Mythic World | Constructed World |
| Star Wars | Constructed World | Mythic World |
| Harry Potter | Hidden World | Constructed World (wizarding detail) |
| Dune | Constructed World | Mythic World (prophecy, messiah) |
| The Matrix | Hidden World | Broken World (real world is ruined) |
| Broken Earth trilogy | Broken World | Constructed World |
