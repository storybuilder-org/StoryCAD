A detailed worldbuilding example, like the fictional world of Aethelgard, involves core elements such as its unique Geography (floating isles, crystal rivers), History (The Sundering, Era of Sky-Kings), Cultures (Aether-touched Elves, Deep-Dwell Dwarves), unique Magic/Tech (Aether-weaving, Sky-ships), Societies (Council of Isles, Guilds), unique Flora/Fauna (Glow-moss, Cloud-Serpents), and a distinct Economy/Resources (Aether Crystals, Sky-Silk), all woven together to create a consistent, lived-in setting, much like Tolkien's Middle-earth. 
Here's a filled-in example for Aethelgard:
World Name: Aethelgard
Geography & Cosmology: A planet with shattered continents, now a vast expanse of floating islands (Aether-Isles) above a perpetual, glowing mist (The Veil). Gravity is localized around large landmasses; smaller islands drift. Rivers of liquid light (Aether-Streams) flow between them.
History & Lore:
The Sundering: An ancient cataclysm that shattered the world into floating isles.
Era of Sky-Kings: Powerful mages who ruled from colossal sky-citadels.
The Silence: A period after the Sky-Kings vanished, leading to smaller, independent island-states.
Cultures & Peoples:
Aether-Touched Elves (Ael'Dra): Inhabit high-altitude isles, masters of air magic, ethereal, long-lived.
Deep-Dwell Dwarves (Glimmerkin): Live within larger, stable isles, expert miners of Aether Crystals, gruff but honorable.
Human Skyfarers (Free-Traders): Nomadic human clans who pilot sky-ships, pragmatic traders and explorers.
Magic & Technology:
Magic (Aether-weaving): Drawing power from ambient Aether, used for flight, communication, energy.
Technology: Steam-powered sky-ships (using refined Aether fuel), intricate clockwork devices, crystal-based communication relays.
Society & Politics: A loose Confederation of Isles (Ael'Dra & Glimmerkin alliances) vs. the independent Free-Trader Fleets. Guilds (Aether-Miners, Cartographers) hold significant power.
Flora & Fauna: Glow-moss (bioluminescent), Whisper-Vines (communicate through vibrations), Sky-Whales (gentle giants migrating through The Veil), Razor-Hawks (predatory birds).
Daily Life & Economy:
Food: Cultivated sky-vegetables, farmed Cloud-Fish, preserved Sky-Whale blubber.
Trade Goods: Aether Crystals (power source), Sky-Silk (lightweight fabric), rare herbs from deep isles.
Culture: Emphasis on navigation, storytelling (oral tradition of the Sky-Kings), reverence for the skies.
Conflict & Themes: Scarcity of stable land, resource wars over Aether Crystals, the mystery of The Sundering, clash between tradition and progress. 
This example shows how diverse elements (geography, culture, magic) interrelate, creating a believable world with built-in story potential.
