## Tab 2: Physical Worlds

Click **+ Add World** and enter:

### Entry 1: Land of Oz

**Name:** Land of Oz

**Geography:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> A vast continent surrounded by the Deadly Desert, which turns any living thing that touches it to sand. The land is divided into four quadrants by color: Munchkin Country (blue, east), Winkie Country (yellow, west), Gillikin Country (purple, north), and Quadling Country (red, south). The Emerald City sits at the center where all regions meet. Notable features include the Haunted Forest, the Dainty China Country, and the underground Nome Kingdom.

**Climate:**
> Generally temperate and pleasant year-round. Each region maintains consistent weather suited to its magical nature. Rain falls when needed, crops grow abundantly, and harsh winters are unknown. The magical nature of Oz ensures comfortable conditions for its inhabitants.

**NaturalResources:**
> Abundant magical resources. The Emerald City is built entirely of green gems. The Nome Kingdom beneath Oz contains vast precious metal and gemstone deposits. Food grows readily without much cultivation. Water is plentiful except in the Deadly Desert.

**Flora:**
> Unusual plants abound. Trees may bear lunch pails or dinner plates. Flowers in some regions are sentient and can be aggressive. The forests contain both familiar and magical vegetation, with some plants possessing unusual properties or behaviors.

**Fauna:**
> Animals can speak and often possess human-level intelligence. Notable species include Winged Monkeys, the Cowardly Lion's kind, Kalidahs (tiger-headed bears), and the Hungry Tiger. Many creatures serve as companions or adversaries to travelers.

**Astronomy:**
> The sky of Oz appears similar to Earth's, though the land's magical nature means celestial events may have different significance. The sun rises and sets normally, but the enchanted nature of the land means time may pass differently than in the outside world.

## Tab 3: People/Species

Click **+ Add Species** twice and enter:

### Entry 1: Munchkins

**Name:** Munchkins

**PhysicalTraits:**
> Small in stature compared to typical humans, generally no taller than three feet. Round faces, cheerful expressions. Traditionally dress in blue, the color of their country. Both men and women may wear pointed hats with bells.

**Lifespan:**
> Effectively immortal within Oz due to the land's enchantment that prevents death. Munchkins do not age past maturity and cannot die of natural causes while in Oz.

**Origins:**
> Native inhabitants of eastern Oz, present since the land's enchantment by the fairy Lurline. They have always occupied Munchkin Country and developed their agricultural society over countless generations.

**SocialStructure:**
> Agricultural society with local governance. Wealthy farmers hold social standing. Community-oriented with strong traditions of hospitality and gratitude. Historically subject to the rule of witches, now loyal to Princess Ozma.

**Diversity:**
> Variation exists in wealth and occupation—farmers, craftspeople, merchants. Some Munchkins live in towns while others maintain rural homesteads. All share the characteristic small stature and blue color preference.

### Entry 2: Winkies

**Name:** Winkies

**PhysicalTraits:**
> Human-sized inhabitants of western Oz. Traditionally associated with the color yellow, which dominates their clothing and architecture. Physically ordinary humans in most respects.

**Lifespan:**
> Like all Oz inhabitants, effectively immortal due to the land's enchantment. They do not age or die of natural causes.

**Origins:**
> Native to the western region of Oz. Their history was shaped by long enslavement under the Wicked Witch of the West, from which they were liberated by Dorothy and her companions.

**SocialStructure:**
> Now ruled benevolently by the Tin Woodman, whom they embraced after liberation. Skilled metalworkers with guild traditions. Their culture is recovering from generations of witch domination.

**Diversity:**
> Primarily craftspeople and artisans, particularly skilled in metalwork. The Winkie tinsmith who created the Tin Woodman's body represents their highest craft traditions.

## Tab 4: Cultures

Click **+ Add Culture** and enter:

### Entry 1: Emerald City Culture

**Name:** Emerald City Culture

**Values:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> Truth, beauty, and harmony above all. Loyalty to the ruler of Oz. Hospitality to visitors. The color green symbolizes hope and prosperity. Material comfort is valued but not at the expense of kindness.

**Customs:**
> All residents and visitors must wear green-tinted spectacles while in the city (originally to maintain the illusion of emerald buildings). Formal audiences with the ruler follow strict protocols. Citizens maintain their homes and shops in immaculate condition.

**Taboos:**
> Questioning the Wizard's power (during his rule) was forbidden. Violence within city limits is prohibited. Removing the green spectacles while in the city was once a serious offense.

**Art:**
> Architecture is the primary art form—every building gleams with green decoration. Music and pageantry accompany official ceremonies. Gardens are maintained as living art throughout the city.

**DailyLife:**
> Comfortable urban existence. Citizens have jobs but work is not burdensome. Food is plentiful. The Guardian of the Gates controls access to the city. Streets are clean and well-maintained.

**Entertainment:**
> Royal balls and ceremonies. Garden parties. Musical performances. Visits to the palace for audiences with the ruler. Social gatherings in the city's many public spaces.

## Tab 5: Governments

Click **+ Add Government** and enter:

### Entry 1: Monarchy of Oz

**Name:** Monarchy of Oz

**Type:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> Absolute monarchy centered in the Emerald City. The ruler of Oz commands the entire land, though regional areas have local governance. Princess Ozma is the rightful hereditary ruler, a fairy princess restored to her throne after usurpation.

**PowerStructures:**
> Central authority flows from Princess Ozma in the Emerald City. Regional rulers (like the Tin Woodman in Winkie Country) govern their territories but defer to Ozma. The Good Witch Glinda serves as powerful ally and advisor. Magic users hold significant informal power.

**Laws:**
> Death is abolished by royal decree—no one dies in Oz. Magic is regulated. The Deadly Desert serves as natural border control. Visitors from the outside world are rare but welcomed. Justice is administered fairly under Ozma's rule.

**ClassStructure:**
> Relatively egalitarian under Ozma's benevolent rule. The royal court holds highest status. Regional differences exist but no formal class system oppresses citizens. Magical beings and animated constructs are accepted as full citizens.

**ForeignRelations:**
> Allied with Glinda's southern domain. At peace with all four regional countries. Historical conflicts with the Wicked Witches (now defeated). Ongoing tension with the Nome King's underground kingdom, which periodically threatens invasion.

## Tab 6: Religions

Click **+ Add Religion** and enter:

### Entry 1: Oz Folk Beliefs

**Name:** Oz Folk Beliefs

**Deities:**
> No formal deities are worshipped, as magic's reality requires no faith. The fairy Lurline, who enchanted the land, is revered as the source of Oz's magical nature. Good witches and fairies are respected as powerful beings but not worshipped.

**Beliefs:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> Magic is real and pervasive—this is simply accepted fact, not faith. Good magic protects and heals; wicked magic harms and controls. Goodness ultimately triumphs over wickedness. The land itself is alive with enchantment.

**Practices:**
> No formal worship exists, as magic's reality requires no faith. Gratitude toward good witches and fairies is common. Celebration of liberation from wicked rulers. Respect for the natural magical order of the land.

**Organizations:**
> Glinda the Good maintains a court in Quadling Country that functions as both political and spiritual center. No formal priesthood exists. The Emerald City palace serves ceremonial functions under Ozma's rule.

**CreationMyths:**
> The fairy Lurline enchanted the land of Oz during her travels, granting it magic and removing death. Before this enchantment, Oz was an ordinary land. Lurline's blessing transformed it into a fairy country where magic is the natural order.

## Tab 7: History

**FoundingEvents:**
> The fairy Lurline enchanted the land of Oz during her travels, granting it magic and removing death. The land developed into four color-coded regions with the Emerald City at the center. King Pastoria ruled the unified land until the Wizard's arrival.

**MajorConflicts:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> The Wicked Witches' tyranny over Munchkin Country (East) and Winkie Country (West). The Wizard's usurpation of the throne and deception of the populace. The Nome King's repeated attempts to conquer Oz and steal its magic. Various magical beings' schemes against the throne.

**Eras:**
> The Age of Lurline's Enchantment (origins). The Pastoria Dynasty (legitimate rule). The Wizard's Reign (usurpation through deception). The Interregnum (various rulers). The Restoration of Ozma (current golden age under the rightful fairy princess).

**TechnologicalShifts:**
> Oz relies on magic rather than technology. The Wizard introduced some mechanical devices (his hot air balloon, various tricks), but these were anomalies. The Tin Woodman represents a unique fusion of mechanical construction and magical animation.

**LostKnowledge:**
> The witch Mombi's transformation magic that hid Princess Ozma's identity was eventually overcome but represented dangerous lost arts. The Wicked Witches possessed knowledge that died with them. Some fairy magic remains beyond mortal understanding.

## Tab 8: Economy

**EconomicSystem:**
> Post-scarcity magical economy. Money exists but is largely unnecessary. Citizens have what they need through magic, abundant natural resources, and the lack of death or serious illness. Exchange occurs but deprivation is unknown.

**Currency:**
> Exists but rarely needed. Emeralds and other gems serve as currency when required. Most transactions involve barter, gift-giving, or simple sharing. The concept of poverty is essentially foreign to Oz.

**TradeRoutes:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> Limited external trade due to the Deadly Desert barrier that surrounds Oz. Internal trade exists between the four regions—Winkies' metalwork travels east, Munchkin agricultural products move to the city. The Yellow Brick Road serves as the primary trade route connecting regions.

**Professions:**
> Farmers (especially Munchkins), metalworkers (Winkies), craftspeople, merchants, palace servants, and guardians. The Guardian of the Gates holds a unique position. Many pursue crafts for satisfaction rather than survival.

**WealthDistribution:**
> Relatively egalitarian. The royal court and regional rulers enjoy greater comfort but no one suffers want. The magical abundance of the land eliminates extreme poverty. Status comes from skill, service, and magical ability rather than wealth accumulation.

## Tab 9: Magic/Technology

**SystemType:** Magic

**Source:**
> The fairy Lurline's original enchantment of the land provides the foundation for all magic in Oz. Individual practitioners (witches, wizards, fairies) tap into or manipulate this ambient magical energy. Some magic comes from artifacts imbued with power.

**Rules:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> Magic is pervasive and reliable in Oz. The land's enchantment prevents death for all inhabitants. Transformation magic can change beings but is typically reversible. Geographic magic creates natural boundaries (Deadly Desert). Power levels vary among practitioners—Glinda exceeds most others.

**Limitations:**
> Even powerful magic has rules and limits. The Silver Shoes can be lost. Transformation magic can be undone by more powerful magic. Wicked magic can be defeated by good magic. The Deadly Desert cannot be crossed even by magical means. Death, while abolished, can occur to those who leave Oz.

**Cost:**
> Magic in Oz generally has no physical cost to practitioners, unlike darker magic systems. However, magical artifacts can be lost or stolen. Wicked magic often corrupts its users. The Golden Cap limits its bearer to three commands of the Winged Monkeys.

**Practitioners:**
> Glinda the Good (most powerful living practitioner). Princess Ozma (fairy magic, rightful ruler). The Wizard (actually a fraud who used trickery, now learning real magic). Mombi (transformation specialist, defeated). Various regional witches and magical beings.

**SocialImpact:**
> Magic shapes every aspect of life in Oz. Animated beings (Scarecrow, Tin Woodman, Jack Pumpkinhead) are accepted as full citizens. Death's abolition fundamentally changes society—no aging, no natural death, no urgency. Geography itself is magical, with talking trees, dangerous forests, and enchanted regions.
