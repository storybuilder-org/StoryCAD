# Gestalt to Axis Mapping

**Created:** 2026-01-19
**Purpose:** Map each of the 8 World Type gestalts to the 6 orthogonal axes

---

## The 6 Axes (Reference)

### 1. Ontology (What exists)
- **Mundane** – Only consensus reality
- **Supernatural** – Magic, gods, spirits exist
- **Scientific Speculative** – Non-current but naturalistic phenomena
- **Hybrid** – Magic and science both operative
- **Symbolic** – Reality follows mythic/dream logic

### 2. World Relation (Relation to our reality)
- **Primary World** – Our world
- **Divergent Earth** – Alternate / secret history
- **Secondary World** – Fully separate reality
- **Layered** – Hidden realms overlapping ours
- **Multiversal** – Many coexisting worlds

### 3. Rule Transparency (How rules behave)
- **Explicit Rules** – Knowable, consistent systems
- **Implicit Rules** – Consistent but unexplained
- **Capricious** – Inconsistent or will-based
- **Symbolic Rules** – Meaning overrides causality

### 4. Scale of Difference
- **Cosmetic** – Surface changes only
- **Structural** – Social, historical, technological shifts
- **Cosmological** – Different laws of reality

### 5. Agency Source (What drives change)
- **Human-Centric** – People drive events
- **Nonhuman Intelligences** – Aliens, gods, AI
- **Fate / Providence** – Destiny, prophecy
- **Systemic Forces** – Technology, magic, ecology

### 6. Tone Logic (How the world feels)
- **Rational** – Cause and effect dominate
- **Mythic** – Archetypal, meaningful
- **Whimsical** – Playful, light
- **Dark / Entropic** – Decay, struggle
- **Transcendent** – Spiritual, transformative

## Gestalt-to-Axis Mappings

### 1. Consensus Reality

> The world works as expected. No hidden rules.
> → Literary realism, most mainstream fiction.

| Axis | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ontology | Mundane | No speculative elements |
| World Relation | Primary World | Our world |
| Rule Transparency | Explicit Rules | Real-world physics/society |
| Scale of Difference | Cosmetic | No divergence from reality |
| Agency Source | Human-Centric | People drive all events |
| Tone Logic | Rational | Cause and effect |

**Important nuance:** "Consensus" refers to a *specific group or subculture*. Every Consensus Reality story still requires worldbuilding - it's building a particular slice of reality with its own rules, norms, and insider knowledge. The consensus of Wall Street differs from the consensus of suburbia differs from the consensus of a police precinct.

**Subculture examples:**
- **Police procedural**: 87th Precinct, Harry Bosch - precinct culture, hierarchy, procedures
- **Medical procedural**: ER, House - hospital culture, medical ethics
- **Legal procedural**: Grisham novels - courtroom rules, firm politics
- **Wall Street/Finance**: Bonfire of the Vanities, House of Cards - excess, money's moral logic
- **Suburbia**: Big Little Lies, Stepford Wives, Virgin Suicides - conformity, hidden dysfunction

**Example works:**
- Updike's Rabbit series
- 87th Precinct novels (Ed McBain)
- Jesse Stone movies
- Most literary fiction, thrillers, mysteries

### 2. Enchanted Reality

> Our world, but reality is porous. The impossible is accepted, not analyzed.
> → Magical realism, slipstream.

| Axis | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ontology | Supernatural | Magic exists but isn't systematized |
| World Relation | Primary World | Our world |
| Rule Transparency | Implicit Rules | Consistent but unexplained |
| Scale of Difference | Cosmetic | Surface integration of impossible |
| Agency Source | Systemic Forces | Magic as environmental force |
| Tone Logic | Symbolic | Meaning over mechanism |

**Example works:**
- *One Hundred Years of Solitude* (García Márquez)
- *Like Water for Chocolate* (Esquivel)
- *The House of the Spirits* (Allende)
- *Beloved* (Morrison)
- *Pan's Labyrinth* (film)

---

### 3. Hidden World

> Our world + concealed layers. Discovery is dangerous or costly.
> → Urban fantasy, secret history.

| Axis | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ontology | Supernatural | Magic/beings exist hidden |
| World Relation | Layered | Hidden realms overlap ours |
| Rule Transparency | Explicit Rules | Magic has rules (kept secret) |
| Scale of Difference | Structural | Parallel society exists |
| Agency Source | Nonhuman Intelligences | Hidden powers affect events |
| Tone Logic | Rational or Dark | Discovery has consequences |

**Example works:**
- Harry Potter series (Rowling)
- Dresden Files (Butcher)
- American Gods (Gaiman)
- Men in Black (film)
- The Matrix (film) - technological variant

---

### 4. Divergent World

> History or conditions forked. Logic remains rational.
> → Alternate history, near-future SF.

| Axis | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ontology | Mundane or Scientific Speculative | No magic; science may differ |
| World Relation | Divergent Earth | Historical fork |
| Rule Transparency | Explicit Rules | Rational extrapolation |
| Scale of Difference | Structural | Different society/technology |
| Agency Source | Human-Centric or Systemic | History shaped by decisions or tech |
| Tone Logic | Rational | Cause and effect from divergence |

**Example works:**
- *The Man in the High Castle* (Dick)
- *11/22/63* (King)
- Steampunk (aesthetic variant)
- Cyberpunk - *Neuromancer* (Gibson)
- *The Handmaid's Tale* (Atwood)

---

### 5. Constructed World

> Entirely built reality. Coherent internal logic.
> → Epic fantasy, secondary-world SF.

| Axis | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ontology | Supernatural or Hybrid | Magic often present |
| World Relation | Secondary World | Fully separate from ours |
| Rule Transparency | Explicit Rules | Author-defined systems |
| Scale of Difference | Cosmological | Different laws of reality |
| Agency Source | Variable | Depends on story |
| Tone Logic | Variable | Depends on story |

**Example works:**
- *A Song of Ice and Fire* (Martin)
- Discworld (Pratchett) - whimsical tone
- *The Stormlight Archive* (Sanderson)
- *Dune* (Herbert) - SF variant
- *Star Wars* - science fantasy

---

### 6. Mythic World

> Reality follows meaning, not causality. Fate, prophecy, archetypes dominate.
> → Tolkien, Le Guin (Earthsea), myth retellings.

| Axis | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ontology | Symbolic | Reality serves narrative meaning |
| World Relation | Secondary World | Often; or mythologized Primary |
| Rule Transparency | Symbolic Rules | Meaning overrides causality |
| Scale of Difference | Cosmological | Fundamental reality differs |
| Agency Source | Fate / Providence | Prophecy, destiny, divine will |
| Tone Logic | Mythic | Archetypal significance |

**Example works:**
- *The Lord of the Rings* (Tolkien)
- *Earthsea* series (Le Guin)
- *The Once and Future King* (White)
- Greek mythology retellings (*Circe*, *Song of Achilles*)
- *The Chronicles of Narnia* (Lewis)

---

### 7. Estranged World

> Reality is unfamiliar and unsettling. Rules exist but resist intuition.
> → Hard SF, surrealism, New Weird.

| Axis | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ontology | Scientific Speculative or Symbolic | Alienating physics or logic |
| World Relation | Variable | Often Secondary or far-future Primary |
| Rule Transparency | Explicit but Alien | Rules exist but feel wrong |
| Scale of Difference | Cosmological | Fundamental strangeness |
| Agency Source | Systemic or Nonhuman | Inhuman forces dominate |
| Tone Logic | Dark / Entropic or Rational | Alienation, discomfort |

**Example works:**
- *Solaris* (Lem)
- *Annihilation* (VanderMeer)
- *Perdido Street Station* (Miéville)
- *Blindsight* (Watts)
- Lovecraft's cosmic horror
- *2001: A Space Odyssey* (Clarke/Kubrick)

---

### 8. Broken World

> Systems have failed. Survival replaces progress.
> → Post-apocalyptic, dystopian.

| Axis | Value | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Ontology | Mundane or Scientific Speculative | Usually no magic |
| World Relation | Divergent Earth | Future fork from our world |
| Rule Transparency | Explicit Rules | Harsh but consistent |
| Scale of Difference | Structural | Society collapsed/transformed |
| Agency Source | Human-Centric or Systemic | Survival; oppressive systems |
| Tone Logic | Dark / Entropic | Decay, struggle, scarcity |

**Example works:**
- *The Road* (McCarthy)
- *Mad Max* series
- *1984* (Orwell)
- *The Walking Dead*
- *Station Eleven* (Mandel)
- *A Canticle for Leibowitz* (Miller)

## Observations

### Clear Mappings
- **Consensus Reality** and **Enchanted Reality** are cleanest - all axes map clearly
- **Hidden World** and **Divergent World** are fairly clean
- **Broken World** is a specialized variant of Divergent World (future-focused, entropic tone)

### Variable Mappings
- **Constructed World** has the most flexibility - it's defined by being "fully built" rather than by specific axis values
- **Estranged World** spans SF and weird fiction with different ontologies

### Hybrid Possibilities
Some works combine gestalts:
- *Lord of the Rings* = Constructed + Mythic
- *Star Wars* = Constructed + Mythic (science fantasy)
- *Harry Potter* = Hidden World (could argue Constructed for wizarding world detail)

### Design Implication
The UI could:
1. Let users select a primary gestalt
2. Optionally select a secondary influence
3. Advanced mode shows axes for fine-tuning
