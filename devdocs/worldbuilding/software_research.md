# Worldbuilding Software Research

**Research Date:** January 13, 2026
**Purpose:** Understand the landscape of dedicated worldbuilding software, their organizational structures, key features, and integration with story writing.

---

## Executive Summary

The worldbuilding software market is divided into several categories:

1. **Dedicated Worldbuilding Platforms** (World Anvil, LegendKeeper, Kanka) - Purpose-built for worldbuilding with structured templates
2. **Writer-Focused Worldbuilding Tools** (Campfire, Plottr) - Designed primarily for authors with integrated manuscript features
3. **General-Purpose Tools Adapted for Worldbuilding** (Obsidian, Notion, Scrivener) - Flexible platforms customized via templates/plugins
4. **Free/Open-Source Options** (Fantasia Archive, Notebook.ai) - Lower-cost alternatives with varying feature sets

Key findings:
- Most tools organize content around **entity types** (Characters, Locations, Items, etc.)
- **Hierarchical organization** and **inter-linking** are universal features
- **Interactive maps** and **timelines** are premium differentiators
- Integration with manuscript writing varies widely
- Pricing ranges from free to ~$300/year for premium tiers

## Detailed Tool Analysis

### 1. World Anvil

**URL:** https://www.worldanvil.com/

**Target Audience:** Authors, tabletop gamers, worldbuilding hobbyists, game masters, RPG players

#### Category/Taxonomy Structure

World Anvil uses a **template-based article system** with 25+ predefined article types organized into categories:

**Characters & Beings:**
- Character (named creatures, people, NPCs, gods, sentient beings)
- Species (sapient species, animals, plants, monsters)
- Ethnicity

**Geography & Places:**
- Geography/Location (natural places: mountains, rivers, continents, planets, galaxies, dimensions)
- Settlement (organized living places: hamlets to space stations)
- Building (constructions, monuments, streets, rooms)

**Organizations & Society:**
- Organization (groups united for any reason: covens, armies, guilds, countries)
- Military Formation
- Title (ranks, distinctions, honorifics)

**Items & Technology:**
- Item (objects, tools, clothes, weapons, food)
- Technology/Science (artifacts, inventions, machines)
- Material (resources, raw materials)
- Vehicle

**Magic & Conditions:**
- Spell (magical spells, psionics, superpowers)
- Condition (diseases, mutations, transformations, RPG statuses)
- Physical/Metaphysical Law

**Culture & Knowledge:**
- Tradition/Ritual (celebrations, festivals, superstitions, dress codes)
- Language
- Document (books, audio recordings, letters, illustrations)

**Events & Narrative:**
- Military Conflict
- Plot
- Prose
- Report

**Meta/Generic:**
- Generic article (catch-all)
- Custom Templates (Grandmaster+ feature)

#### Organizational Features

- **Categories:** Hierarchical tree structure for organizing articles; serve dual purpose of private organization and public table of contents
- **Tags:** Cross-referencing system for quick filtering
- **Variables:** Reusable content (character names, tooltips, BBCode/CSS layouts)
- **Cross-Association:** Articles link to each other (e.g., character articles as parents of other characters, owners of organizations)
- **Family Trees:** Interactive visualization of character relationships
- **Relationship Panels:** Display how characters relate to each other

#### Notable Features

- **Interactive Maps:** Pins, lines, labels, markers, layers on uploaded map images; nest maps within maps
- **Timelines:** Historical event tracking across multiple timelines
- **Manuscripts:** Full novel-writing software integrated with worldbuilding (HTML export)
- **RPG Campaign Manager:** Campaign management tools for tabletop games
- **Notebooks:** Note-taking with starred notes, icons, color-coded sections (Oct 2025 update)
- **Custom Templates:** Create personalized article structures (Grandmaster+)
- **Co-authoring:** Multiple contributors with permission controls
- **World Dashboard:** Overview of world content and statistics

#### Pricing Model

| Tier | Monthly | Annual | Lifetime | Key Features |
|------|---------|--------|----------|--------------|
| Freeman (Free) | $0 | $0 | - | 2 worlds, basic templates, articles, maps |
| Master | ~$4.50 | $54 | - | Core worldbuilding features, ideal for beginners |
| Grandmaster | ~$8.25 | $97 | $650 | Custom templates, RPG systems, 100 subscriber slots |
| Sage | ~$25 | $299 | - | 20 co-authors, 1000 subscribers, white-labeling, custom URL |

#### Story Writing Integration

- Built-in manuscript editor
- Manuscripts link to world articles
- Export to HTML (copy-paste to Word/Google Docs)
- Sidebar reference to world content while writing

### 2. Campfire (Campfire Write / Campfire Pro)

**URL:** https://www.campfirewriting.com/

**Target Audience:** Fiction authors (primarily fantasy and sci-fi), writers who prioritize narrative over world-sharing

#### Category/Taxonomy Structure

Campfire uses a **modular system** with 17 distinct modules, each focusing on a specific aspect:

**Narrative Modules:**
- Manuscript (primary writing interface)
- Arcs (character development arcs)
- Timeline (plotting and historical events)

**Character Modules:**
- Characters (profiles with traits, goals, backstories)
- Relationships (character connections and dynamics)

**World Modules:**
- Locations (custom panels for images, histories, stats)
- Maps (interactive pins, nested maps, Inkarnate integration)
- Encyclopedia (wiki-style articles for any topic)
- Cultures
- Religions
- Species
- Languages (lexicon builder with scripts and dictionaries)
- Magic (systems, mechanics, costs)
- Items
- Systems (custom worldbuilding frameworks)
- Philosophies

**Research Module:**
- Research (reference material organization)

#### Organizational Features

- **Panels:** Modular, movable, resizable content blocks within each entry
- **Cross-Linking:** Tag story elements directly in manuscript
- **Sidebar Access:** Reference notes while drafting
- **Custom Categories:** User-defined organization within modules
- **World Builder's Pack:** Preset categories for species, cultures, languages, religions, philosophies, magic, items

#### Notable Features

- **Index Card View:** Organize chapters visually like cards on a corkboard
- **Word Count Goals:** Track writing statistics per chapter
- **Manuscript Tagging:** Link world elements directly in prose
- **Real-Time Collaboration:** Share with editors, commenters, viewers
- **Spoiler Protection:** Hide panels from collaborators
- **Multiple Viewing Modes:** Different access levels for collaborators
- **Offline Access:** Desktop application works without internet
- **Mobile Apps:** iOS and Android support
- **Inkarnate Integration:** Direct map linking

#### Pricing Model

**Modular Pricing (pay only for what you need):**

| Module | Monthly |
|--------|---------|
| Manuscript only | $2.00 |
| All Modules | $12.00-$12.50 |

**Bundled Options:**
- Annual: $125
- Lifetime: $375 (20% discount for all modules)

#### Story Writing Integration

**Strongest integration of all tools reviewed:**
- Manuscript is a core module, not an afterthought
- Direct tagging of story elements in text
- Index card outlining
- Word count tracking
- Sidebar reference panel
- Export capabilities for drafts

**Philosophy:** "If the writing comes first and the world second, use Campfire."

### 3. LegendKeeper

**URL:** https://www.legendkeeper.com/

**Target Audience:** Game masters, storytellers, TTRPG players

#### Category/Taxonomy Structure

LegendKeeper uses a **flexible wiki-based system** with four core pillars:

**The Four Pillars:**
1. **Wiki** - Infinite page nesting, no predefined categories
2. **Maps (Atlas)** - Interactive, nested map system
3. **Whiteboards (Boards)** - Free-form visual organization
4. **Timelines** - Chronicle view, Gantt charts, calendars

#### Organizational Features

- **Infinite Page Nesting:** User-defined hierarchical structure (no forced templates)
- **Page Templates:** Reusable designs stored in dedicated folder
- **Auto-Linking:** Automatically discovers and links related pages
- **Tags:** Flexible categorization across pages
- **Properties:** Custom fields attached to pages
- **Featured Images:** Visual identifiers for pages
- **Wiki Shortcuts:** Drag-and-drop navigation
- **Slash Commands:** Keyboard-driven editing

#### Notable Features

- **Interactive Maps:** Up to 14K pixels and 100MB uploads
- **Map Nesting:** Navigate from world to region to town to dungeon
- **Custom Map Pins:** Different icons, colors, borders
- **Visibility Controls:** Filter pins by tags, hide player-facing elements
- **Timelines:** Custom calendar systems, event linking, multiple timeline views
- **Whiteboards:** Real-time multiplayer, Page Cards, Board Cards
- **Asset Management:** Centralized image/map organization with folders
- **Offline Support:** Local storage sync when offline
- **Full Export:** HTML or JSON export (never paywalled)
- **Access Controls & Secrets:** Gradually reveal lore to players
- **Meter Blocks:** Progress bars, gauges, token pools (Dec 2025)
- **Spotify Integration:** Music blocks in wiki pages

#### Pricing Model

| Tier | Monthly | Annual |
|------|---------|--------|
| Free Trial | 14 days | - |
| Standard | $9 | $90 |
| Commercial | Higher tier | Additional features |

#### Story Writing Integration

- Wiki can serve as story bible
- Timeline for plot planning
- Real-time collaboration for co-authors
- Export to HTML/JSON for external use
- No built-in manuscript editor (use alongside writing tools)

## Key Insights for StoryCAD Integration

### Common Patterns to Consider

1. **Entity-Based Organization:** Most tools organize around character, location, item, organization, event types
2. **Hierarchical Nesting:** All tools support some form of parent-child relationships
3. **Cross-Linking:** Wiki-style linking between entities is universal
4. **Templates with Prompts:** Structured templates with guiding questions aid creation
5. **Relationship Tracking:** Character relationships and entity connections are valued features
6. **Timeline Integration:** Historical events and plot timelines are premium features
7. **Map Integration:** Interactive maps linking to world content differentiate premium tools

### Story-Writing Integration Approaches

1. **Built-in Manuscript (World Anvil, Campfire):** Full writing within same tool
2. **Export-Focused (Plottr):** Design for handoff to dedicated writing tools
3. **Reference Sidebar (Campfire):** Access worldbuilding while writing
4. **Separate Tools (Most):** Worldbuilding as reference for external writing app

### Gaps in Current Market

1. **AI-Assisted Worldbuilding:** Only Kanka (Bragi) offers meaningful AI assistance
2. **Story-World Consistency:** Limited tools for ensuring story matches world rules
3. **Character Arc Integration:** Weak connection between character development and worldbuilding
4. **Scene-Level World Reference:** Most tools work at chapter/story level, not scene level
