## Tab 2: Physical Worlds

Click **+ Add World** and enter:

### Entry 1: Land of Oz

**Name:** Land of Oz

**Geography:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> A vast continent surrounded by the Deadly Desert, which turns any living thing that touches it to sand. The land is divided into four quadrants by color: Munchkin Country (blue, east), Winkie Country (yellow, west), Gillikin Country (purple, north), and Quadling Country (red, south). The Emerald City sits at the center where all regions meet. Notable features include the Haunted Forest, the Dainty China Country, and the underground Nome Kingdom.

**Climate:**
> Generally temperate and pleasant year-round. Each region maintains consistent weather suited to its magical nature. Rain falls when needed, crops grow abundantly, and harsh winters are unknown. The magical nature of Oz ensures comfortable conditions for its inhabitants.

**Flora/Fauna:**
> Unusual plants and creatures abound. Trees may bear lunch pails or dinner plates. Animals can speak and often possess human-level intelligence. Notable species include Winged Monkeys, the Cowardly Lion's kind, Kalidahs (tiger-headed bears), and the Hungry Tiger. Flowers in some regions are sentient and can be aggressive.

**Resources:**
> Abundant magical resources. The Emerald City is built entirely of green gems. The Nome Kingdom beneath Oz contains vast precious metal and gemstone deposits. Food grows readily without much cultivation. Water is plentiful except in the Deadly Desert.

**Challenges/Hazards:**
> The Deadly Desert forms an impassable barrier. The Haunted Forest contains dangerous creatures. Various witches and magical beings pose threats. The Fighting Trees block certain paths. Transformation magic can trap unwary travelers.

**Unique Features:**
> Magic works consistently and powerfully. Death is nearly unknown among natives. Inhabitants include both flesh-and-blood beings and animated objects (Scarecrow, Tin Woodman, Jack Pumpkinhead). Multiple underworlds and pocket dimensions connect to the main land.

## Tab 3: People/Species

Click **+ Add Species** twice and enter:

### Entry 1: Munchkins

**Name:** Munchkins

**Physical Characteristics:**
> Small in stature compared to typical humans, generally no taller than three feet. Round faces, cheerful expressions. Traditionally dress in blue, the color of their country. Both men and women may wear pointed hats with bells.

**Society/Culture:**
> Agricultural society, peaceful and hardworking farmers. Deeply grateful to anyone who helps them. Governed by local leaders but ultimately subject to Oz's ruler. Celebrate liberation from the Wicked Witch of the East as a major holiday.

**Abilities/Limitations:**
> No special magical abilities themselves, though they live in a magical land. Skilled craftspeople, particularly in farming and simple manufacturing. Limited military capability, historically vulnerable to witch domination.

**Role in World:**
> The Munchkins represent the eastern population of Oz. They are the first inhabitants Dorothy encounters and serve as the audience's introduction to Oz's wonders. Their gratitude and hospitality establish Oz as a friendly land.

**Notable Members:**
> Boq (a wealthy Munchkin farmer), the Mayor of Munchkinland, various leaders who welcome Dorothy after her house kills the Wicked Witch of the East.

### Entry 2: Winkies

**Name:** Winkies

**Physical Characteristics:**
> Human-sized inhabitants of western Oz. Traditionally associated with the color yellow. Physically ordinary humans in most respects.

**Society/Culture:**
> Long enslaved by the Wicked Witch of the West, they developed a culture of fearful obedience. After liberation, they embraced the Tin Woodman as their ruler. Skilled metalworkers—they repaired the Tin Woodman multiple times.

**Abilities/Limitations:**
> Excellent craftsmen, particularly in metalwork. No inherent magical abilities. Their skills allowed them to repair and maintain the Tin Woodman.

**Role in World:**
> Represent the western population. Their enslavement and liberation illustrates the stakes of witch rule and the importance of the heroic quest.

**Notable Members:**
> The Winkie tinsmith who originally created the Tin Woodman's body; various soldiers of the Wicked Witch's army (who served under duress).

## Tab 4: Cultures

Click **+ Add Culture** and enter:

### Entry 1: Emerald City Culture

**Name:** Emerald City Culture

**Values:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> Truth, beauty, and harmony above all. Loyalty to the ruler of Oz. Hospitality to visitors. The color green symbolizes hope and prosperity. Material comfort is valued but not at the expense of kindness.

**Customs:**
> All residents and visitors must wear green-tinted spectacles while in the city (originally to maintain the illusion of emerald buildings). Formal audiences with the ruler follow strict protocols. Citizens maintain their homes and shops in immaculate condition.

**Taboos:**
> Questioning the Wizard's power (during his rule) was forbidden. Violence within city limits is prohibited. Removing the green spectacles while in the city was once a serious offense.

**Art:**
> Architecture is the primary art form—every building gleams with green decoration. Music and pageantry accompany official ceremonies. Gardens are maintained as living art throughout the city.

**Daily Life:**
> Comfortable urban existence. Citizens have jobs but work is not burdensome. Food is plentiful. Entertainment and leisure are common. The Guardian of the Gates controls access to the city.

**Entertainment:**
> Royal balls and ceremonies. Garden parties. Musical performances. Visits to the Wizard (during his tenure) were both terrifying and thrilling spectacles.

## Tab 5: Governments

Click **+ Add Government** and enter:

### Entry 1: Monarchy of Oz

**Name:** Monarchy of Oz

**Structure:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> Absolute monarchy centered in the Emerald City. The ruler of Oz commands the entire land, though regional areas have local governance. Princess Ozma is the rightful hereditary ruler, though the throne was usurped by the Wizard and later by various antagonists before her restoration.

**Leaders:**
> Princess Ozma (rightful ruler, a fairy princess). Previously: The Wizard of Oz (usurper who ruled through deception), King Pastoria (Ozma's father, deposed). The Good Witches Glinda (South) and the Witch of the North serve as powerful allies to the throne.

**Laws:**
> Death is abolished by royal decree—no one dies in Oz. Magic is regulated. The Deadly Desert serves as natural border control. Visitors from the outside world are rare but welcomed.

**Relations:**
> Allied with Glinda's southern domain. At peace with the Munchkins, Winkies, Gillikins, and Quadlings. Historical conflicts with the Wicked Witches (now defeated). Ongoing tension with the Nome King's underground kingdom.

**Conflicts:**
> Recurring threats from the Nome King. Various magical beings attempt conquest. The original conflict involved the Wizard's usurpation of the throne from the infant Ozma.

## Tab 6: Religions

Click **+ Add Religion** and enter:

### Entry 1: Oz Folk Beliefs

**Name:** Oz Folk Beliefs

**Core Beliefs:**
> Magic is real and pervasive—this is simply accepted fact, not faith. Good magic protects and heals; wicked magic harms and controls. The fairy Lurline created Oz and enchanted it with immortality. Goodness ultimately triumphs over wickedness.

**Practices:**
> No formal worship, as magic's reality requires no faith. Gratitude toward good witches and fairies. Celebration of liberation from wicked rulers. Respect for the natural magical order.

**Leadership:**
> Glinda the Good serves as both political and spiritual authority in the South. The Good Witch of the North provides guidance in her region. No formal priesthood exists.

**Sacred Sites:**
> Glinda's palace in Quadling Country. The Emerald City's throne room. The spot where Dorothy's house landed (marking the Wicked Witch of the East's defeat).

**Rituals:**
> Celebrations when evil is defeated. Formal ceremonies at the Emerald City court. The wearing of green spectacles was once quasi-religious in nature.

## Tab 7: History

**Origins:**
> The fairy Lurline enchanted the land of Oz during her travels, granting it magic and removing death. The land developed into four color-coded regions with the Emerald City at the center. King Pastoria ruled until the Wizard arrived.

**Major Events:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> The Wizard's arrival (via balloon from Omaha) and usurpation of the throne. The infant Ozma hidden by the witch Mombi. Rise of the Wicked Witches in the East and West. Dorothy's first visit and defeat of both Wicked Witches. Discovery and restoration of Princess Ozma. Multiple invasions by the Nome King.

**Conflicts:**
> The Wicked Witches' tyranny over Munchkin Country and Winkie Country. The Nome King's repeated attempts to conquer Oz. Various magical beings' schemes against the throne.

**Turning Points:**
> Dorothy's house killing the Wicked Witch of the East—the beginning of liberation. The Wizard's departure and exposure as a humbug. Ozma's restoration as rightful ruler. Glinda's sealing of Oz from outside interference.

**Legacy:**
> Oz transformed from a land divided under witch tyranny to a unified, peaceful fairy kingdom. Dorothy becomes a permanent resident. The land is eventually made invisible and inaccessible to protect it from the outside world.

## Tab 8: Economy

**System:**
> Post-scarcity magical economy. Money exists but is largely unnecessary. Citizens have what they need through magic, abundant natural resources, and the lack of death or serious illness.

**Trade:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> Limited external trade due to the Deadly Desert barrier. Internal trade exists between regions—Winkies' metalwork, Munchkin agriculture, Emerald City crafts. Barter is common when exchange occurs.

**Resources:**
> Inexhaustible. Food grows readily. The Emerald City's gems. Nome Kingdom minerals (contested). Magical items. Clean water throughout the land.

**Classes:**
> Relatively egalitarian. The royal court holds highest status. Regional differences (Winkies were enslaved, Munchkins free farmers). No poverty in the traditional sense—magical abundance eliminates want.

**Currency:**
> Exists but rarely needed. Emeralds and other gems serve as currency when required. Most transactions involve barter or simple giving.

## Tab 9: Magic/Technology

**System Type:** Pure Magic

**Rules/Limitations:** *(Expand for screenshot)*
> Magic is pervasive and reliable in Oz. The land's enchantment prevents death. Transformation magic (turning people into objects/animals) is common but can be reversed. Geographic magic (like the Deadly Desert) provides natural boundaries. Power levels vary—Glinda is more powerful than most, the Wicked Witches had specific domains.

**Sources:**
> Inherent magical nature of the land (Lurline's enchantment). Magical artifacts (Silver/Ruby Shoes, Golden Cap, Magic Belt). Innate abilities of witches and fairies. Learned magic available to some practitioners.

**Practitioners:**
> Glinda the Good (most powerful). The Wizard (actually a fraud using technology and trickery). Princess Ozma (fairy magic). Various witches, both good and wicked. Mombi (transformation specialist).

**Artifacts:**
> Silver Shoes (later ruby)—transport wearer anywhere. Golden Cap—commands Winged Monkeys three times. Magic Belt (Nome King's)—grants any wish. Powder of Life—animates inanimate objects.

**Impact:**
> Magic shapes every aspect of life. Animated beings (Scarecrow, Tin Woodman) are accepted citizens. Death's abolition fundamentally changes society. Geography itself is magical (talking trees, dangerous forests).

**Limitations:**
> Even powerful magic has rules. The Silver Shoes can be lost. Transformation magic can be undone. Wicked magic can be defeated by good magic. The Deadly Desert cannot be crossed by magical means.
