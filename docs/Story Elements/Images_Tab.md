## Images Tab

The Images tab attaches pictures to a story element: a casting photo for a character, a location snapshot for a setting, or reference and mood art for a scene. It appears on Character, Setting, and Scene elements, and on Notes forms.

### Adding a picture

Click **Add Picture** and choose an image file from your computer. PNG, JPEG, and WebP files work. You can add as many pictures to an element as you want.

### The gallery

Each picture appears as a thumbnail tile with three controls:

- **Caption**: a text field under the thumbnail. Type a description and it is saved with the outline.
- **Enlarge**: opens the picture at full size. Clicking the thumbnail itself does the same thing.
- **Remove**: deletes the picture from the element.

### Viewing a picture full size

Enlarge opens the picture in a window titled with its file name, with your caption below it. Click **Close** to return to the tab.

### How pictures are stored

Pictures are saved inside the outline file (`.stbx`), not as separate files on disk. They stay with the outline when you move it, email it, or restore it from a backup, so there are no loose image files to keep track of. When you add a picture whose long edge is larger than 2048 pixels, StoryCAD scales the stored copy down to 2048 pixels; smaller pictures are stored unchanged. This keeps an illustrated outline small enough to open and save quickly.

### Pictures in reports

Printed and PDF reports can include your pictures as an appendix. See [Print Reports](../Reports/Print_Reports.html).
